Climate Model Descriptions (SSP1-2.6)

1. ACCESS-ESM1-5 (Australia)

Institution: CSIRO

An Earth System Model integrating atmosphere, ocean, land surface, and biogeochemical processes, ideal for studying carbon-climate feedbacks and long-term climate projections.

2. BCC-CSM2-MR (China)

Institution: Beijing Climate Center

Medium-resolution model emphasizing improved monsoon simulations and regional climates across East Asia.

3. CanESM5 (Canada)

Institution: CCCma

Advanced treatment of carbon cycle and aerosol-cloud interactions; strong Arctic performance.

4. CESM2 (USA)

Institution: NCAR

Comprehensive Earth system components including dynamic vegetation and biogeochemical cycles, offering high-resolution simulations.

5. CMCC-ESM2 (Italy)

Institution: CMCC

Simulates full climate dynamics with carbon feedbacks and ocean biogeochemistry.

6. CNRM-CM6-1 (France)

Institution: CNRM

Improved cloud physics and ocean-atmosphere coupling, used for regional projections across Europe and Africa.

7. GFDL-ESM4 (USA)

Institution: NOAA GFDL

Features advanced interactive carbon and nitrogen cycles, enabling ecosystem-climate feedback studies.

8. GISS-E2-1-G (USA)

Institution: NASA GISS

Improved aerosols and atmospheric dynamics; supports historical trend and urban/regional impact studies.

9. TaiESM1 (Taiwan)

Institution: Academia Sinica

Earth system model with dynamic land/sea/ice components and 100 km resolution; strong on regional variability.

10. UKESM1-0-LL (UK)

Institution: UK Met Office

Full biogeochemical ESM with robust simulations of atmosphere-ocean-land interactions and climate-carbon feedbacks.

11. HadGEM3-GC31-LL (UK)

Institution: UK Met Office

Supports high-resolution atmospheric convection and ocean processes; key for regional UK and global simulations.

12. INM-CM5-0 (Russia)

Institution: INM RAS

Focuses on ocean circulation and sea ice dynamics; used for high-latitude climate projection studies.

13. MIROC6 (Japan)

Institution: MIROC Consortium

Advanced cloud physics and improved ocean mixing for reliable future and paleo climate simulations.

15. MRI-ESM2-0 (Japan)

Institution: Meteorological Research Institute
